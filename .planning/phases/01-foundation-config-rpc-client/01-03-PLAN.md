<objective>
Build `bastion/rpc/client.py`: a thin, async-first JSON-RPC client over `httpx.AsyncClient` with a single retry/backoff wrapper every method routes through, correct cursor pagination for `getSignaturesForAddress`, and thin sync wrappers for one-shot CLI calls. Domain-blind transport — pubkeys/signed-bytes in, public JSON out; no keys, no funds.

Purpose: `rpc/client.py` is imported by the funder/sweeper (Phase 3) and the monitor (Phase 6). Its retry budget, pagination correctness, and fee-commitment default are inherited by fund-moving code, so they must be right from the start.

RPC contract notes (from RESEARCH.md, authoritative):
- getSignaturesForAddress caps at 1000 results/call; page backward with before=<oldest sig of prev page>; stop when a page returns < limit. before/until are exclusive; both accept None.
- getFeeForMessage defaults commitment to "finalized" — override to "confirmed" here (Pitfall 4).
- sendTransaction-class calls get a tighter retry budget (Helius free tier ~1/sec).

<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Local Process (rpc client) → External Service (Helius RPC) | This is the External Service Client boundary; responses and rate-limit signals are attacker-influenceable if the endpoint is malicious or MITM'd |
| Rate-limit budget → free-tier quota | Uncontrolled retries can self-inflict a throttle/wedge against the shared quota |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Severity | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-01-07 | Denial of Service | retry/backoff wrapper | high | mitigate | Bounded exponential backoff, Retry-After-aware, ~30s cap, then raise RpcRateLimitError — never retry indefinitely; test `test_retry_budget_exhaustion_raises_typed_error` |
| T-01-08 | Tampering | trusting RPC response shape / pagination stream | medium | mitigate | Typed parsing raises RpcError on malformed JSON-RPC; pagination terminates on a short page (no infinite loop) — test `test_get_signatures_terminates_on_short_page` |
| T-01-09 | Denial of Service | a malicious endpoint returning unbounded full (==limit) pages forever | low | accept | The endpoint is user-chosen via SOLANA_RPC (compromised-`.env` == local access, not a remote attacker); real Helius history is finite so the short-page terminator fires. Residual risk documented; not mitigated at the transport layer |
| T-01-10 | Information Disclosure | logging a signed transaction blob in send_raw | medium | mitigate | send_raw never logs the base64 blob at info level; typed errors carry no payload bytes |
</threat_model>
